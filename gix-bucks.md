# GIX Bucks: The TECHIN 510 Project Economy


## Rules

### Starting Capital

Every student receives **100 GIX Bucks** at the start of the quarter.

### Development Fee

When proposer and developer are matched, they **negotiate a development fee**:

- **Suggested range:** 10-40 GIX Bucks
- The proposer pays the fee from their 100-buck starting capital
- The developer receives the fee as additional investment capital
- The agreed fee is recorded in the project repo's `SPEC.md`

**What affects the fee:**
- Project complexity (more features → higher fee)
- Stack difficulty (unfamiliar stack → developer may charge more)
- AI feature requirement (adds complexity → higher fee)
- Developer confidence (experienced developer may charge more or less depending on interest)

### Investment Round

During demo presentations, every student distributes their **remaining GIX Bucks** as investments:

**Investment rules:**
- You **cannot** invest in your own proposed project
- You **cannot** invest in the project you developed
- No minimum investment per project — spread across many or concentrate on a few
- Investment is public — your classmates will see where you put your money

### Net Profit Calculation

For each project:

```
Net Profit = Total Investment Received - 100
```

- If net profit is **positive** → the project earned bonus points
- If net profit is **negative or zero** → no bonus (no penalty either)

### Bonus Points

Positive profits are normalized and become your bonus points of this course.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I negotiate a fee of 0?**
A: Yes, but it signals that the proposer doesn't value the developer's work. Consider at least a nominal fee (5-10 bucks) to create a real economic relationship.

**Q: Can I invest in a project and then change my mind?**
A: No. Investments are final once submitted at Demo Day. Choose carefully.

**Q: What if all projects have negative profit?**
A: Then no one gets bonus points. This would mean every project's investment was less than its development cost.

**Q: Is the GIX Bucks bonus enough to change my grade?**
A: It depends on how well you do.
